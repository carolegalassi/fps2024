What Is a Funeral Program?

A funeral program is a printed booklet or card distributed to attendees at a memorial or funeral service. It serves as both an order of service and a personal tribute, often including photos, biographical information, poems, and acknowledgments. Funeral programs provide structure to the ceremony and offer a tangible memory for guests to take home.

Key Elements of a Funeral Program

  • Cover with the loved one's name, dates, and photo
  • Order of service or ceremony schedule
  • Obituary or life story
  • Readings, poems, or scriptures
  • Photos and personal messages
  • Acknowledgments and thank you notes
  • Details about the reception or repast

Funeral Program Styles and Formats

Funeral programs come in many styles, from simple single-page handouts to elaborate multi-page booklets. The design you choose can reflect the personality and preferences of your loved one. Below is a comparison of common funeral program formats:

Format Description Best For
Single Fold (Bi-fold) A sheet folded in half, creating four panels for content. Traditional services, concise information
Trifold A sheet folded into thirds, offering six panels for more details. Detailed programs, additional photos
Booklet (Multi-page) Multiple sheets folded and stapled, ideal for extended tributes. Large gatherings, extensive life stories

Designing a Personalized Funeral Program

The best funeral programs are those that capture the unique spirit of the person being honored. Consider incorporating favorite colors, meaningful quotes, or images that reflect their passions. Many families choose to use templates or work with professional designers to ensure a polished result. Printing and Distribution

Once your funeral program is designed, you can print it at home, at a local print shop, or through an online service. Be sure to print enough copies for all attendees, plus extras for those unable to attend. Distribute programs at the entrance to the service or place them on seats before guests arrive.
