Welcome to The Funeral Program Site. If you need to create a funeral program online, the hardest part is usually deciding where to start. This guide breaks the process into five easy options, so you can choose the path that matches your timeline and your comfort level.

Option one is a downloadable template. This is ideal when you want a clean, professional layout without designing from scratch. You simply replace the sample text, add photos, confirm details, and save a file that’s ready to print.

Option two is editing in a browser. This works well when you want quick changes without installing software. It can also make review easier when family members need to check spelling, dates, or service details before you finalize.

Option three is a digital program. Instead of printing, you share it by link or QR code so guests can view it on any device. This is especially helpful for virtual services, travel-heavy guest lists, or paperless preferences.

Option four is done-for-you design and printing. If you want the least stress, you provide the content and photos and the formatting is handled for you, which helps avoid last-minute layout surprises.

Option five is a hybrid approach: professional layout plus a print-ready file. It’s a great fit when you want a polished design but prefer to print locally or at home.

The fastest way to move forward is to place the anchors first: name, dates, service location and time, and one clear photo. Once the anchors are set, the rest of the content is much easier to add without reworking your layout.

The five easy methods (and who they’re best for)

1) Download a template and edit it on your computer

Best for families who want control over wording and photos without building a layout from scratch. The design structure is already set, so you can focus on accuracy and tone.

2) Edit in a browser for quick collaboration

Best when you want to avoid installing software and need fast review. This can be especially helpful when multiple people need to confirm details before you finalize.

3) Create a digital program for link or QR sharing

Best when guests are remote or you prefer paperless sharing. Digital programs can be opened on phones and computers and distributed instantly.

4) Use done-for-you design and printing

Best when you want the least stress and the cleanest finish. You provide the content, and the formatting and printing are handled for you to avoid last-minute layout issues.

5) Hybrid: professional layout + a print-ready file

Best when you want a polished design but need flexibility in where you print. This option lets you print locally, at home, or with a preferred printer—without losing a professional look.

A simple finish strategy

Place the anchors first: name, dates, service time/location, and one clear photo. Then add the obituary and order of service. Save acknowledgements and optional readings for last. This order prevents reformatting and keeps you moving.
