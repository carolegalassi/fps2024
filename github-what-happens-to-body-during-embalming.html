What Happens To Body During Embalming

Body embalming is a process that has been used for centuries to preserve and prepare the deceased for viewing, funeral services, and burial. Understanding what happens to the body during embalming can help families make informed decisions and ease concerns about this important step in the funeral process. This page offers a clear, step-by-step overview of body embalming, including the science, procedures, and reasons behind each stage.

Understanding the Body Embalming Process

Embalming is a specialized procedure performed by licensed funeral professionals. The main goal is to temporarily preserve the body, slow down natural decomposition, and create a natural, peaceful appearance for viewing. The process involves several key steps, each designed to address specific aspects of preservation and presentation.

Key Steps in Body Embalming

Step Purpose Details
Sanitization Clean and disinfect the body The body is washed with a germicidal solution to remove bacteria and prepare for further steps.
Arterial Embalming Preserve tissues and slow decomposition A preservative solution is injected into the arteries, replacing blood and circulating through the body.
Cavity Embalming Treat internal organs Body cavities are aspirated and filled with concentrated chemicals to prevent internal decay.

Why Is Embalming Performed?

Body embalming is not always required by law, but it is often chosen for public viewings, open-casket funerals, or when there will be a delay before burial or cremation. Embalming helps preserve the body, enhances appearance, and provides families with the opportunity for a final goodbye in a dignified setting. The process also allows for transportation of the body over long distances if needed.

What Happens to the Body?

After sanitization, the arterial embalming process begins. Funeral professionals locate a major artery, usually the carotid, and inject a formaldehyde-based solution. This solution travels through the vascular system, replacing blood and preserving tissues. The body is gently massaged to ensure even distribution. Next, cavity embalming addresses the internal organs by removing fluids and gases and replacing them with a strong preservative. Finally, the body is washed again, features are set, and cosmetics may be applied to restore a natural look.
