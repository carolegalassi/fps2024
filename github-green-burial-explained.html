Green Burial Explained: A Complete Guide

Green burial is an environmentally conscious alternative to traditional burial and cremation. As more families seek meaningful, sustainable ways to honor loved ones, green burial has gained attention for its focus on simplicity, natural materials, and minimal environmental impact. This page provides a comprehensive overview of green burial, including its core principles, benefits, and how it compares to other burial options. Whether you are planning ahead or supporting a loved one, understanding green burial can help you make informed, thoughtful choices.

What is Green Burial?

Green burial, sometimes called natural burial, is a method of interment that avoids chemicals, non-biodegradable materials, and energy-intensive processes. The goal is to allow the body to return to the earth naturally, supporting environmental sustainability and reducing the carbon footprint. Green burials typically use biodegradable caskets, shrouds, or simple containers, and avoid embalming fluids or concrete vaults. Cemeteries that offer green burial often preserve natural landscapes, encouraging native plants and wildlife.

Key Features of Green Burial

  • No embalming with toxic chemicals
  • Use of biodegradable caskets or shrouds
  • No concrete vaults or liners
  • Natural or conservation-focused cemetery grounds
  • Minimal environmental impact

Why Choose Green Burial?

Families choose green burial for a variety of reasons. Some are motivated by environmental values, wishing to minimize their ecological footprint. Others appreciate the simplicity and natural beauty of a green burial site, which often resembles a nature preserve more than a traditional cemetery. Green burial can also be more affordable, as it avoids the costs of embalming, metal caskets, and vaults. For many, it is a way to leave a legacy of stewardship and respect for the earth.
