Understanding the Trifold Brochure Layout

A trifold brochure is created by folding a single sheet of paper twice to form three panels. This layout is popular for programs because it offers six distinct sections for information, photos, and graphics. The key to a professional finish is folding each panel evenly and in the correct order. Here is a quick overview of the typical trifold layout:

Panel Purpose Tips
Front Cover Title, event name, date Keep it simple and inviting
Inside Panels Order of service, photos, messages Use clear headings and sections
Back Panel Contact info, acknowledgments Include a thank you note or directions

Step by Step: How to Fold a Trifold Brochure

  1. Prepare Your Brochure: Print your brochure on quality paper. Make sure the design is aligned for a trifold layout.
  2. Identify the Panels: Lay the brochure face up. The rightmost panel is usually folded in first, followed by the left panel over the top.
  3. Score the Fold Lines: For the cleanest folds, lightly score the paper along the fold lines using a bone folder or the back of a butter knife and a ruler. This helps prevent cracking and ensures a straight crease.
  4. Make the First Fold: Fold the right panel in toward the center. Align the edge carefully and press down the crease.
  5. Make the Second Fold: Fold the left panel over the right, lining up the edges. Press the crease firmly.
  6. Finish and Inspect: Run your finger or a bone folder along both creases to sharpen them. Check that all edges are aligned and the brochure closes flat.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Folding without scoring can lead to uneven or cracked creases.
  • Not aligning the panels can cause the brochure to look sloppy.
  • Using paper that is too thick or thin may affect the quality of the fold.
