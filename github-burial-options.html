Understanding Your Burial Options

There are several burial options available today, each offering different benefits and considerations. Whether you are planning ahead or making arrangements for a loved one, understanding these choices can help you make an informed decision that honors your values and wishes.

Traditional Ground Burial

Traditional ground burial remains the most common choice for many families. This option typically involves a casket, a burial plot in a cemetery, and a headstone or marker. Families may choose between private or public cemeteries, and additional services such as graveside ceremonies are often available. Ground burial provides a permanent place for loved ones to visit and remember.

Cremation

Cremation has grown in popularity due to its flexibility and affordability. After cremation, remains can be kept in an urn, scattered in a meaningful location, or even incorporated into memorial objects. Some families choose to bury the urn in a cemetery or place it in a columbarium. Cremation can also be combined with traditional services, allowing for both a funeral and a final resting place.

Green and Eco-Friendly Burials

Eco-friendly or green burials are designed to minimize environmental impact. These options may include biodegradable caskets or shrouds, natural burial grounds without embalming chemicals, and planting trees or wildflowers as living memorials. Green burials appeal to those who wish to leave a lighter footprint and support conservation efforts.
