Burial at Sea Basics

Burial at sea is a time-honored tradition that offers a unique and meaningful way to say farewell to a loved one. Whether chosen for personal, spiritual, or environmental reasons, this option provides families with a memorable ceremony surrounded by the vastness and tranquility of the ocean. This guide covers the burial at sea basics, including legal requirements, planning tips, and essential resources to help you navigate the process with confidence and respect.

Understanding Burial at Sea

Burial at sea involves the respectful disposal of human remains in the ocean, typically performed from a boat or ship. This practice is recognized and regulated in many countries, including the United States, and can be performed for both cremated and intact remains. Many families choose this option for its natural setting, sense of adventure, or to honor a loved one’s connection to the sea.

There are two main types of burial at sea: full body burial and burial of cremated remains. Each type has specific requirements and procedures, which are important to understand before making arrangements. Working with a licensed provider or funeral director experienced in sea burials can help ensure all regulations are met and the ceremony is conducted with dignity.

Legal and Environmental Considerations

Before planning a burial at sea, it is essential to be aware of the legal requirements. In the United States, the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) oversees sea burials and mandates that full body burials must take place at least three nautical miles from land and at a minimum depth of 600 feet. Cremated remains may be scattered at sea with fewer restrictions, but notification to the EPA is still required within 30 days of the ceremony.

Environmental stewardship is a key aspect of burial at sea. Only biodegradable materials should be used, and all items placed in the water must comply with environmental guidelines. This ensures that the ceremony honors both the deceased and the ocean environment.

Planning the Ceremony

Planning a burial at sea involves several important steps. Families should select a reputable provider, coordinate with a captain or funeral director, and discuss the type of service desired. Ceremonies can be religious or secular, and may include readings, music, and the participation of family and friends. Some families choose to record or livestream the event for those unable to attend in person.

It is also important to consider the logistics of transportation, weather, and accessibility for guests. Many providers offer packages that include all necessary arrangements, making the process easier for grieving families.
