What Is a Funeral Brochure?

A funeral brochure, sometimes called a memorial program or order of service, is a printed document distributed at a funeral or memorial service. It typically includes the order of events, poems, scriptures, photos, and personal tributes. The brochure serves as both a guide for attendees and a lasting memento for family and friends. Thoughtful design and content can help celebrate the life and legacy of your loved one.

Key Elements of a Funeral Brochure

While every funeral brochure is unique, most include several essential elements. Below is a table outlining the most common sections found in a well-designed funeral brochure:

Section Purpose Tips
Cover Page Features the name, photo, birth and passing dates of the loved one Choose a clear, high-quality photo and simple design
Order of Service Lists the sequence of events, readings, and music Keep it concise and easy to follow
Obituary & Tributes Shares the life story and personal remembrances Include heartfelt stories and quotes

Design and Personalization Tips

Personalizing a funeral brochure can make the service more meaningful. Consider these tips to create a unique and memorable program:

  • Use your loved one’s favorite colors, flowers, or themes in the design
  • Include multiple photos throughout the brochure, not just on the cover
  • Add meaningful poems, scriptures, or song lyrics that reflect their personality
  • Ask family and friends to contribute short tributes or memories
  • Choose easy-to-read fonts and maintain a clean, organized layout

Printing and Distribution

Once your funeral brochure design is complete, you can print copies at home, at a local print shop, or through an online service. Use quality paper stock for a professional look and consider how many copies you will need for guests. Some families also choose to share a digital version by email or on a memorial website, making it accessible to those who cannot attend in person.
