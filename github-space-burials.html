What Are Space Burials?

Space burials involve sending a small portion of cremated remains, DNA, or a symbolic keepsake into space via a commercial rocket. This can mean a brief journey to the edge of space, a lasting orbit around Earth, or even a voyage to the Moon or deep space. The concept was first realized in the late 20th century, and since then, several companies have made it possible for families to honor loved ones with a cosmic memorial. Space burials are not only a tribute to those who loved science fiction or astronomy, but also a way to express the limitless nature of human remembrance.

How Space Burials Work

The process typically begins with selecting a service provider that specializes in space memorials. Families choose the type of mission, such as suborbital flight, Earth orbit, lunar, or deep space. The remains or token are securely packaged in a special capsule, which is then integrated into a commercial rocket payload. After launch, families may receive tracking information, certificates, and sometimes even video footage of the mission. Some providers offer ceremonies at the launch site, allowing loved ones to witness the event in person or via live stream.

Why Choose a Space Burial?

Families and individuals are drawn to space burials for many reasons. For some, it is a way to honor a loved one’s passion for space, science, or adventure. For others, it is a symbol of the human spirit’s desire to explore and transcend boundaries. Space burials can be a meaningful choice for those who want a non-traditional memorial, or for anyone seeking a truly unique and memorable tribute. The process is dignified, respectful, and often accompanied by personalized touches such as certificates, launch attendance, and digital keepsakes.
