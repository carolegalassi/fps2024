Understanding Cremation Urn Sizing

When asking what size cremation urn do I need, the answer depends on the amount of cremated remains, also known as ashes, you will be placing in the urn. The industry standard is that one pound of body weight before cremation yields approximately one cubic inch of ashes. For example, a person who weighed 180 pounds will require an urn that holds at least 180 cubic inches.

It is important to consider if you will be sharing the ashes among family members, keeping all the ashes together, or planning to scatter some. This will affect the urn size you need. There are also keepsake urns and companion urns for different needs.

Cremation Urn Size Chart

Urn Type Capacity (Cubic Inches) Recommended For
Standard Adult Urn 180 - 220 Most adults
Keepsake Urn 1 - 50 Sharing or small portion
Companion Urn 350 - 400 Two adults

How to Calculate the Right Urn Size

To determine the urn size you need, use this simple formula: 1 pound of body weight = 1 cubic inch of urn capacity. It is always recommended to choose an urn slightly larger than the minimum requirement to ensure all ashes will fit comfortably. If you are unsure, consult with your funeral director or crematory for guidance.

Other Factors to Consider

  • Will the urn be displayed, buried, or placed in a columbarium niche?
  • Do you want to divide the ashes among several urns or keepsakes?
  • Are there size restrictions for your chosen resting place?
  • Would you prefer a decorative, personalized, or eco-friendly urn?
