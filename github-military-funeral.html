Military Funeral: Traditions, Honors, and Planning Guide

A military funeral is a solemn tribute to honor the service and sacrifice of veterans and active duty service members. These ceremonies are rich with tradition and symbolism, offering families and communities a meaningful way to say farewell to their loved ones. This guide will help you understand the essential elements of a military funeral, eligibility requirements, ceremonial honors, and resources for planning.

What Is a Military Funeral?

A military funeral is a ceremony conducted to honor a deceased member of the armed forces. These funerals are available to eligible veterans, retirees, and active duty personnel. The service typically includes a flag-draped casket, the playing of Taps, a rifle volley, and the folding and presentation of the United States flag to the next of kin. Each branch of the military has its own traditions, but all military funerals are designed to show respect and gratitude for the individual’s service.

Eligibility for Military Funeral Honors

Not every service member automatically qualifies for full military honors. Eligibility is based on service records, discharge status, and other criteria. The following table outlines the basic eligibility and honors provided:

Eligibility Honors Provided Required Documents
Active Duty Service Member Full Honors (flag, Taps, rifle team, escort) DD Form 214, Death Certificate
Veteran (Honorable Discharge) Flag, Taps, Honor Guard DD Form 214
Retired Military Full Honors, Special Requests Retirement Orders, DD Form 214

Key Elements of a Military Funeral

Military funerals are marked by several time-honored traditions. The United States flag is carefully draped over the casket and later folded with precision. The playing of Taps, a haunting bugle call, signals a final farewell. An honor guard may perform a rifle volley, and the folded flag is presented to the next of kin. Some services may include a military chaplain, color guard, or ceremonial escort. Each element is designed to show respect and gratitude for the individual’s service and sacrifice.

How to Arrange a Military Funeral

To arrange a military funeral, contact your funeral director or the local Veterans Affairs office. You will need to provide proof of military service, such as the DD Form 214. The funeral director can coordinate with the appropriate military branch to arrange for an honor guard and other ceremonial elements. Planning ahead can help ensure all honors are rendered and that the ceremony reflects the wishes of the family and the deceased.
