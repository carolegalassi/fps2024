What Is a Funeral Program?

A funeral program is a printed document distributed at a memorial or funeral service. It provides attendees with an outline of the ceremony, shares details about the deceased, and often includes readings, songs, and personal tributes. Funeral programs serve as both a guide for the service and a lasting memento for family and friends.

Key Elements of a Funeral Program

While every funeral program is unique, most include several core elements. Here is a table summarizing the most common sections and their purposes:

Section Purpose Tips
Cover Page Displays the name, photo, birth and death dates of the deceased Choose a clear photo and simple, elegant fonts
Order of Service Lists the sequence of events, speakers, and music Confirm all names and details with participants
Obituary & Tributes Shares a life story, memories, and personal messages Keep it heartfelt and concise

Designing a Meaningful Funeral Program

When designing a funeral program, consider the personality and preferences of your loved one. Select colors, images, and themes that reflect their life and legacy. Many families choose to include favorite poems, scriptures, or song lyrics. You can create your own layout using word processing software or use a professional template for a polished look.
