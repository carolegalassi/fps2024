What Is Green Burial?

Green burial is an environmentally conscious approach to interment that minimizes the ecological impact of traditional funeral practices. Unlike conventional burials, which often involve embalming chemicals, metal caskets, and concrete vaults, green burial emphasizes natural decomposition and the use of biodegradable materials. This method allows the body to return to the earth in a way that supports conservation and sustainability.

Families choose green burial for a variety of reasons, including environmental stewardship, simplicity, and a desire for a more natural return to the earth. Green cemeteries often preserve native habitats and encourage the growth of local flora, making each burial a contribution to the land's ongoing health.

Key Features of Green Burial

Feature Traditional Burial Green Burial
Casket Material Metal or hardwood Biodegradable wood, wicker, or shroud
Embalming Commonly used Not used or non-toxic alternatives
Grave Liner/Vault Required in most cemeteries Not used

Planning a Green Burial

When planning a green burial, start by researching local cemeteries that offer eco-friendly options. Many areas now have dedicated green burial grounds or hybrid cemeteries with natural sections. Discuss your wishes with family members and funeral professionals to ensure your preferences are honored. Choose biodegradable caskets or shrouds, and consider memorial options such as native plantings or simple markers.

Benefits of Green Burial

Green burial offers several advantages:

  • Reduces chemical and material waste
  • Supports land conservation and restoration
  • Encourages simplicity and natural beauty
  • Often more affordable than traditional burial
  • Provides a lasting legacy of environmental stewardship
