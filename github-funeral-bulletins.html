What Are Funeral Bulletins?

Funeral bulletins, also known as memorial service programs or funeral programs, are printed handouts distributed to guests at a funeral or memorial service. They typically outline the order of events, include biographical details, and feature photos, poems, scriptures, or special messages. These bulletins serve both as a guide for attendees and as a lasting tribute to the life being celebrated.

Key Elements of a Funeral Bulletin

While every funeral bulletin is unique, most include several essential components. Here is a quick overview of what you may want to include:

Section Purpose Tips
Cover Page Displays the name, photo, birth and passing dates Choose a clear, high-quality photo
Order of Service Lists the sequence of events and participants Consult with your officiant or funeral director
Personal Tributes Shares memories, poems, or scriptures Invite family to contribute

How to Create a Funeral Bulletin

Creating a funeral bulletin can feel overwhelming, but with the right resources, it becomes a meaningful and manageable task. Start by gathering essential information and photographs. Decide on the structure and content, then choose a template or design that reflects your loved one’s personality and the tone of the service. Many families use online templates or work with professional designers to ensure a polished result.

Design Tips and Best Practices

When designing your funeral bulletin, keep these best practices in mind:

  • Use clear, legible fonts and avoid cluttered layouts
  • Incorporate meaningful colors, symbols, or themes
  • Proofread all text and double-check names and dates
  • Print a sample copy before making multiples
  • Consider digital versions for remote guests
