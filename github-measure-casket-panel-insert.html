Understanding Casket Panel Inserts

Casket panel inserts are decorative or personalized panels that fit into the lid of a casket. They can be used to display photos, artwork, religious symbols, or meaningful text. Because casket interiors vary by manufacturer and model, it is important to measure the panel area precisely to ensure a perfect fit. An accurate measurement will help you avoid costly mistakes and delays, especially when ordering custom inserts.

Tools You Will Need

  • Flexible tape measure or fabric measuring tape
  • Notepad and pen for recording measurements
  • Camera or smartphone (optional, for reference photos)
  • Helper (optional, for holding tape in place)

Step-by-Step Measurement Process

  1. Open the casket lid fully to access the interior panel area. Make sure the area is well-lit and free of obstructions.
  2. Measure the width of the panel area from left to right at the widest point. Record the measurement in inches or centimeters.
  3. Measure the height from the top edge to the bottom edge of the panel area. Again, record the measurement carefully.
  4. Check for curves or contours. Some casket lids have rounded or arched panels. If so, measure at multiple points and note the shape.
  5. Double-check your measurements to ensure accuracy. If possible, have a second person verify.
  6. Take a reference photo of the panel area with a ruler or tape measure in view. This can help when ordering or designing your insert.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Measuring only the visible area and not the full insert space
  • Forgetting to account for curves or decorative trim
  • Rounding measurements up or down instead of using exact numbers
  • Not verifying measurements with a second person
