Above Ground Burial: Understanding Your Options and Considerations

Above ground burial is an increasingly popular choice for families seeking dignified, lasting memorials for their loved ones. Unlike traditional in-ground burial, above ground options offer unique benefits, different memorial styles, and a variety of personalization opportunities. This page provides a comprehensive overview of above ground burial, including its types, benefits, and important considerations to help you make an informed decision.

What Is Above Ground Burial?

Above ground burial refers to interment options where the deceased is placed in a structure above the earth rather than buried in the ground. These structures, such as mausoleums and columbariums, are designed to provide a secure, respectful, and often visually striking resting place. Above ground burial can be chosen for personal, religious, or environmental reasons and is available in many cemeteries and memorial parks.

Types of Above Ground Burial

Type Description Typical Use
Mausoleum A free-standing building or structure that houses one or more crypts for casketed remains. Families, individuals, or community groups
Columbarium A wall or structure with niches designed to hold urns containing cremated remains. Cremation memorialization
Sarcophagus An above ground stone container, often ornately decorated, used to hold a casket or remains. Historical or cultural memorials

Benefits of Above Ground Burial

Families choose above ground burial for a variety of reasons. Some appreciate the permanence and protection offered by mausoleums, while others value the ability to visit loved ones in an indoor or sheltered environment. Above ground options can also be more environmentally friendly, especially when paired with cremation. Many find comfort in the architectural beauty and legacy that these memorials provide.

  • Protection from the elements and environmental factors
  • Opportunities for family or group memorialization
  • Reduced ground disturbance and potential environmental benefits
  • Personalization with engravings, artwork, and custom designs
  • Year-round accessibility for visitation

Considerations Before Choosing

When considering above ground burial, it is important to review the costs, cemetery regulations, and available options in your area. Mausoleum and columbarium spaces may have different pricing structures than traditional plots. Maintenance fees, personalization choices, and religious or cultural preferences should also be discussed with your funeral provider.
