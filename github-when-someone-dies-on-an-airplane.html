When Someone Dies On An Airplane: What Happens and What To Do

Air travel is a routine part of modern life, but few people consider what happens if someone dies on an airplane. While rare, in-flight deaths do occur, and airlines have established protocols to manage these sensitive situations. This guide explains what happens when someone dies on an airplane, what steps are taken by the crew, and what families and fellow passengers should know.

What Happens When Someone Dies During a Flight?

When a passenger becomes unresponsive or is suspected to have died on an airplane, the flight crew follows strict procedures. Trained crew members will attempt to provide first aid and, if possible, resuscitation. If medical professionals are on board, they may assist. If the person cannot be revived, the crew will notify the captain, who will then inform air traffic control and the airline's ground operations.

Depending on the circumstances, the flight may be diverted to the nearest airport so that local authorities can take over. In some cases, if the death occurs close to the destination or if diversion is impractical, the flight may continue as planned. The deceased is typically moved to a discreet area, such as an empty row or the back of the plane, and covered respectfully.

Airline and Crew Responsibilities

Airlines have protocols in place to handle in-flight deaths with dignity and care. Crew members are trained to manage both the deceased and the emotional needs of other passengers. They will coordinate with ground staff to ensure that authorities and medical personnel are ready upon landing. Documentation and incident reports are completed, and the family or travel companions are given support and information about next steps.

What Families Should Know

If you are traveling with someone who passes away during a flight, the airline will work with you to provide information and assistance. Upon landing, local authorities will take over, and the process for repatriation or local arrangements will begin.
