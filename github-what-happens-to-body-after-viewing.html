What Happens To Body After Viewing

Understanding what happens to body after viewing is an important part of the funeral process for families and loved ones. After the viewing, there are several steps that take place to ensure the deceased is treated with dignity and respect as the next phase of arrangements begins. This page provides a detailed look at the procedures, care, and considerations that occur after a viewing, helping you feel informed and prepared during a difficult time.

What Happens Immediately After a Viewing?

Once the viewing concludes, funeral home staff respectfully guide family and guests out of the room. The body is then carefully prepared for the next step, whether that is burial, cremation, or another service. The casket may be closed at this point, and the deceased is transported to a secure area within the funeral home. Staff ensure all personal items are accounted for and that the body is handled with the utmost care.

Preparation for Final Disposition

Depending on the family’s wishes and the chosen method of disposition, several actions may take place:

  • If burial is planned, the body remains in the casket and is prepared for transport to the cemetery.
  • If cremation is chosen, the body is respectfully transferred to the crematory, following all legal and procedural requirements.
  • Any religious or cultural rituals requested by the family are honored at this stage.

Care and Documentation

Funeral directors and staff complete all necessary documentation, including permits and logs. They also ensure the deceased’s identification is maintained throughout the process. The funeral home may also coordinate with cemetery or crematory staff to confirm arrangements and timing.

Typical Steps After a Viewing

Step Description Who Is Involved
Closing the Viewing Family and guests say final goodbyes, and staff close the casket if needed. Funeral Home Staff, Family
Transport Body is moved to a secure area or vehicle for transfer to the next location. Funeral Home Staff
Final Arrangements Preparation for burial, cremation, or other requested service. Funeral Director, Cemetery or Crematory Staff
